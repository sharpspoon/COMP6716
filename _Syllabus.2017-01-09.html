PresentationS

 

You are required to do a presentation on a topic in software quality assurance.  A list of potential topics is provided below, but you may select any topic as long as I explicitly approve it.  You should plan to work in a team of two (each Outreach student should work individually).

 

6 p.m., Mon 6 Feb, is the deadline for each team to turn in their presentation topic and abstract. After you receive approval of the topic and abstract from me, you can proceed with the project.  Experience has shown that the earlier you submit your prospective topic and abstract, the better the chance that your topic will not have been selected by another team.  It will be your responsibility to come up with an acceptable topic and abstract before the deadline. The abstract should be one paragraph to one page long and must have at least 3 references.  One team member should submit the topic and abstract via email as a PDF or Word file attachment (with a copy to the teammate).  The SUBJECT should include “SQA Presentation” and optionally 5710, 6710, or 6716.  I will confirm receipt by a reply all.

 

You may want to avoid topics that will be discussed in the class unless you are planning to do a detailed presentation of a subtopic.

                                     

The presentations are tentatively scheduled to begin after spring break.  There will be approximately six groups / class days of presentations with five presentations each class period.  For a team of two, each presenter will have about 6 minutes to present with about 3 minutes for Q&A at the end for a maximum of 15 minutes; 6716 Online students and other single presenters should plan for a maximum of 10 minutes.  PowerPoint format is preferred for the presentation along with tool demonstrations as appropriate.  Both team members must contribute in the material preparation and participate in the presentation.  When you send in your topic and abstract, you may also indicate your preference for a presentation group (1 to 6) for your team.  Before the presentation, you need to provide me a copy of your slides.

 

Some principles for presentation preparation:

·       Use bullet points, not a copy of entire paragraph

·       Use large font size and figures

·       Give a brief introduction of context and purposes of presentation

·       Explain purposes of figure, meaning of axes, and terms

·       Make eye contact with audience, talk to them, not just the instructor

·       Rehearse with partner multiple times

·       Use your allotted time fully but do not exceed it

·       Prepare answers for potential questions

·       If possible, use a personal computer for the presentation rather than the podium computer
